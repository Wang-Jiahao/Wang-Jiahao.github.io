Research Interests

We are solving more complicated AI problems with larger datasets, which requires more computation. However, Moore's Law is slowing down. The amount of computation per unit cost is no longer increasing at its historical rate. Running deep neural networks are power hungry so that I want to solve that problem by making efficient AI algorithms and hardware. The goal of my research is to make faster and greener AI everywhere not only on large scale data centers, but also on edge devices, for example, on the smartphones, sensors and Micro controllers. My research interests are machine learning, computer vision and optimization, including model compression, neural architecture search, reinforcement learning, low-level vision tasks, weakly or unsupervised learning and control theory in AI.

  • CF-GAN: Efficient Generative Adversarial Networks

    • [Paper]

    This project studies the neural architecture search (NAS) problem for developing efficient generator networks for image translation and super resolution tasks. We first discover an intact search space of generator networks including three dimensionalities, i.e., path, operator, channel for fully excavating the network performance. To reduce the huge search cost, we explore a coarse-to-fine search strategy which divides the overall search process into three sub-optimization problems accordingly. In addition, a fair supernet training approach is utilized to ensure that all sub-networks can be updated fairly and stably. Experiments results on benchmarks show that we can provide generator networks with better image quality and lower computational costs over the state-of-the-art methods.
